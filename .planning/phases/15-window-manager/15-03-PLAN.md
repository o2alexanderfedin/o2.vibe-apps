<objective>
Build `WindowFrame` — the outer draggable glass chrome. The KEY architectural decision this plan PINS (resolving the checker's fork): the app does NOT mount as a host-tree child of WindowFrame. Instead, WindowFrame mounts — via the `mount.ts` seam `mountApp(instanceId, bodyRef.current, Wrapper)` — a small **Wrapper component** that renders `<AppShell displayName onClose onModify>{createElement(Component)}</AppShell>`. So a SINGLE manager-owned React root (created by mountApp) contains the AppShell + its ⋮ ContextualPrompt (MOD-01..04 wiring) + the app Component together, and `unmountApp(instanceId)` on close tears down ALL of it.

This is the only arrangement where BOTH invariants hold at once:
- the `mountedCount()` zero-leak guarantee (the mandated seam is exercised; close → unmountApp tears down the whole subtree), AND
- the AppShell ⋮ ContextualPrompt MOD-01..04 wiring (AppShell is inside the mounted root, wrapping the Component).

WindowFrame itself (in the HOST tree) is the macOS-style frame: traffic-light titlebar (close/minimize/decorative), app icon + title, a draggable titlebar handle (wired to `useDrag` from Plan 01), and the empty body `<div ref={bodyRef}>` that the Wrapper mounts INTO. WindowFrame consumes `WindowEntry` props from `useWindowManager` (Plan 02) and the manager's focus/minimize/close callbacks.

Also add the CSS: `.desktop` (the isolated window-host container, `isolation: isolate`), `.window-chrome` (glass via Phase 14 `--glass`/`--bord` vars + `display:none` when minimized), `.titlebar-handle`, and `.traffic-light` rules — all neutral-named.

Defends Pitfalls 1/2 (drag via useDrag), 3 (window lives in the isolation:isolate `.desktop`), 4 (minimized = display:none, no compositor layer), 8 (cleanup calls unmountApp), 9 (the manager's `isOpen` is the primary mount guard — Plan 04 — and `document.contains(bodyRef.current)` is the last-resort backstop in this effect), 12 (raise-to-front on body pointerdown does NOT preventDefault, so app `<input>` keeps focus).

Purpose: An opened app renders inside a draggable glass window with shared chrome (WIN-01); pointer-down raises it (WIN-03); minimize hides it via display:none (WIN-04); the frame's lifecycle ties to the React-root teardown (WIN-05).
Output: `src/ui/WindowFrame.tsx` + `src/ui/WindowFrame.test.tsx` + CSS additions in `src/index.css`.
</objective>
